# H1 Seizure-Semiology-Bench: A Clinically-Grounded Benchmark for Video Understanding of Seizure Semiology

**Seizure-Semiology-Bench**, the first comprehensive benchmark designed to evaluate VLM-based understanding of epileptic seizure semiology. Seizure-Semiology-Bench is built on three pillars: (1) a novel, expert-curated dataset of over 400 seizure event videos from more than 400 patients, collected in collaboration with the UCLA Epilepsy Monitoring Unit (EMU); (2) a detailed taxonomy of 41 clinically-critical semiologies, grounded in authoritative medical literature; and (3) a unique 7-level hierarchical task framework that probes VLM capabilities across a spectrum of increasing clinical complexity, from basic sign detection to advanced diagnostic reasoning. Our extensive evaluation of state-of-the-art VLMs on this benchmark reveals a significant "clinical reasoning gap." While current models can identify prominent motor events, they consistently falter on tasks requiring nuanced temporal analysis, precise spatial localization of symptoms, and multi-step clinical inference, performing substantially below the level of human experts. Seizure-Semiology-Bench provides a crucial tool for galvanizing targeted progress in medical AI, setting a new standard for responsible benchmarking with sensitive clinical data, and paving the way for VLMs that can function as reliable, assistive tools for clinicians. The dataset will be made available to the research community under a controlled access policy with a Data Use Agreement (DUA) to ensure patient privacy and ethical compliance
